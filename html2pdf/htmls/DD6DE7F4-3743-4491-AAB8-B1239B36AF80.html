ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

Candidates need to appear for a pre medical test KCET 2014 to qualify.

Indian Students

Domicile and Age Eligibility

  • Candidate seeking admission to KCET should be an Indian citizen including the wards of Jammu and Kashmir.

  • She/he must have attained 17 years of age at the time of admission or should complete 17 years of age on or before 31st December 2014.

Academic Eligibility

The applicants should have passed the qualifying examinations in the manner mentioned below

  • The Higher Secondary Certificate/Indian School Certificate Examination/Karnataka Board/Central Board of Education Examination/PUC or an equivalent examination thereto comprising Physics, Chemistry, Biology and English.

  • The applicant must have passed in the subjects of Physics, Chemistry and Biology individually and must have obtained a minimum of 50% marks taken together in Physics, Chemistry and Biology  at the qualifying examination

  • Applicants belonging to Scheduled Castes/Scheduled Tribes/Other Backward Classes must have passed in the subjects of Physics, Chemistry and Biology individually and must have obtained a minimum of 40% marks taken together in Physics, Chemistry and Biology at the qualifying examination

  • Applicants who have appeared or will be appearing in the qualifying examination in March / April 2014 and whose results have not yet been declared can also apply for admission and appear in the pre medical test, if other eligibility criteria are satisfied.

Entrance Examination Eligibility

Admissions shall be made on the basis of inter-se merit of the candidates qualified at all India entrance exams.

  • Candidates belonging to the open category must secure not less than 50% marks in Physics, Chemistry and Biology taken together at the KCET 2014 examination.

  • Candidates belonging to backward classes must secure not less than 40% marks in Physics, Chemistry and Biology taken together at the examination conducted for KCET 2014.

Application Form Process

Within Karnataka

Applications for KCET 2014 will be made available across schools in Karnataka against a challan that has to be downloaded from the Karnataka Examinations Authority.

  • Download challan (three copies) from the KEA website by entering their name, date of birth and name of the bank where application fees is to be paid.

  • Pay requisite fees to the bank branch which is Rs 650/-. Application fees for Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and Category 1 candidates is Rs. 500/-.

  • Bank will retain a copy of the challan and hand over two copies to the candidates.

  • Candidates are required to hand over one copy to a school or college Principal to obtain the brochure.

Outside Karnataka

Applications for KCET 2014 can be obtained via Speed Post only.

  • Send a self-addressed cloth lined envelope of size 25 cm x 30 cm along with a demand draft.

The Executive Director,

Karnataka Examinations Authority,

Bangalore

  • Demand Draft should be for Rs. 750/- (Rs. 5000/- for Karnataka candidates residing outside India) and should be drawn in favor of “The Executive Director, Karnataka Examinations Authority, Bangalore” payable at Bangalore.

  • The cover in which the Demand Draft and self-addressed envelope are sent should be super scribed with the words “Request for CET-2014 Brochure”. It should be sent to the Executive Director.

Submission of Application Form

  • Application for KCET may be submitted personally to colleges/schools or mailed by speed post with a demand draft as mentioned above.

  • Please retain a copy of the challan.

  • Incomplete application, application with false details or application form not accompanied by a demand draft would be rejected.

SYLLABUS & PATTERN FOR KCET 2014

Syllabus

The syllabus for the combined entrance test for Physics, Chemistry and Biology will be based on syllabus followed by 10+2 Boards/Universities of Karnataka and India.

Exam Pattern

The KCET 2014 exam pattern is different from the other all India medical exams. Instead of one paper, candidates have to appear for three papers over a period of two days. The papers will be for Biology, Physics and Chemistry. Each paper will be for a period of 80 minutes each and each will be for 60 marks.  The details for the exam timings and marks are given in the table below.

Entry after Receiving Verification Number

  • The candidate will have the choice to enter all the options in the discipline to which he/she is eligible.

  • Candidate has to enter the options for all the discipline at once. If the candidate is eligible for multiple disciplines i.e., both for Medical and Dental and/or Farm Science courses / B.Pharma, options has to be exercised for all the discipline in the same entry form.

  • That means, first option may be Medical, second option may also be Medical, third option may be Dental, fourth one may be again Medical, fifth option can be Dental, the sixth may be Farm Science etc and so on.

  • Entering the priority numbers means you are interested to join that particular college and courses.

  • There is no limit for entry of options. A Candidate can enter any number of options as he / she wishes to exercise on his / her own priority.

  • Candidates are advised to enter more number of options to avoid the disappointment of not securing a seat.

  • Candidate’s interest in the discipline, college infrastructure, course, distance from your place, availability of hostel etc may be some of the issues to guide your choices of options.

  • Options recorded in the server on the last day and time of the notified schedule in each round will be frozen and only the frozen options will be considered for allotment of seats. The candidates are advised to take a print out of the option entered by them in each round after the last date and time for entry of options is over.

  • Do not disclose the Secret Keyword / Password with others.
