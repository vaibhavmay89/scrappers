JEE Advanced Opening and Closing Ranks of IITs & ISM, Cutoff 2013

All IITs and ISM Dhanbad declare their JEE Advanced Opening and Closing Ranks after each round of counseling. An Opening Rank is the highest rank of all candidates accepted in a course and Closing Ranks is the lowest rank of all candidates accepted in a course.


For example:

Suppose 20 students apply for a course in an institute with 5 seats. Student with the highest AIR will be accepted in the course first. This will be the Opening Rank for that course in that institute. Student with the second best AIR will then be chosen and so on until all the five seats are full. The rank of the last student accepted in the course will be the Closing Rank for that course.

For General seats, students are chosen from Common Merit List (CML) . For reserved seats, students are chosen from respective category lists according to their category rank. Hence, Opening and Closing Rank for each category differs.

  • Only those students who attain the specified IIT Cut Offs 2014 will be included in the CML and category merit lists for JEE Advanced.

In order to make sure that no seats remain vacant, three rounds of counseling are held. In each subsequent round of counseling, opening and closing ranks come down. You should keep a close eye on the official exam website after each round of counselling to check out the IIT JEE Advanced Opening and Closing Ranks 2014 as soon as they are published.

IIT JEE Opening and Closing Ranks 2014 is expected to be similar to JEE Opening and Closing Ranks 2013. To get a rough idea of where your JEE Advanced All India Rank (AIR) 2014 may take you, check out the final data (consolidating data of first round of counseling, second round of counseling and third round of counseling) on course-wise JEE Advanced 2013 Opening and Closing ranks for all the IITs and ISM Dhanbad:
