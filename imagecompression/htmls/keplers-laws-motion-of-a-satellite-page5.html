Satellites and Planetary Motion

Illustration:

A planet of mass m moves along an ellipse around the sun so that its maximum and minimum distances form the sun are equal to R and r, respectively. Find the angular momentum of this planet relative to the centre of the sun.

Solution:

According to Kepler's second law, the angular momentum of the planet is constant.

.·. mv1R = mv2r or v1R = v2r

If the mass of the Sun is M, conserving total mechanical energy of the system at two given positions we have,

 

-GMm/R + 1/2 mv12 = -(G M m)/r + 1/2 mv22

.·.      GM[1/R - 1/r] = v12/2 + v22/2 or GM[(r-R)/Rr] = v12/2 + v22R2/2r2

.·.      v12= (2GM(R-r) r2)/Rr(R2-r2 ) = 2GMr/R(R+r)

Now, angular momentum = mv1R = m√2GMR/(R+r)
